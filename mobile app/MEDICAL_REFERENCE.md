# 🩺 Medical Reference - CBC Parameters

## Complete Blood Count (CBC) Parameters Reference

This document provides medical reference information for all CBC parameters analyzed by AI BloodScan.

---

## 🔴 Red Blood Cell (RBC) Parameters

### 1. Hemoglobin (HGB)

**Function:** Protein in red blood cells that carries oxygen from lungs to body tissues.

**Units:** g/dL (grams per deciliter)

**Reference Ranges:**
- **Male:** 13.5 - 17.5 g/dL
- **Female:** 12.0 - 15.5 g/dL
- **Children:** 11.0 - 16.0 g/dL (varies by age)

**Clinical Significance:**

| Status | Range | Possible Conditions |
|--------|-------|---------------------|
| **Low** | < 12.0 (F), < 13.5 (M) | Anemia, blood loss, nutritional deficiency, bone marrow disorders |
| **Normal** | Within range | Healthy oxygen-carrying capacity |
| **High** | > 15.5 (F), > 17.5 (M) | Polycythemia, dehydration, lung disease, living at high altitude |

**Symptoms of Abnormality:**
- Low: Fatigue, weakness, pale skin, shortness of breath
- High: Headaches, dizziness, blurred vision

---

### 2. Red Blood Cell Count (RBC)

**Function:** Total number of red blood cells in the blood.

**Units:** ×10⁶/µL (millions per microliter)

**Reference Ranges:**
- **Male:** 4.5 - 5.9 ×10⁶/µL
- **Female:** 4.1 - 5.1 ×10⁶/µL

**Clinical Significance:**

| Status | Possible Conditions |
|--------|---------------------|
| **Low** | Anemia, hemolysis, chronic kidney disease, leukemia |
| **High** | Polycythemia vera, chronic hypoxia, bone marrow disorders |

---

### 3. Hematocrit (HCT)

**Function:** Percentage of blood volume occupied by red blood cells.

**Units:** % (percentage)

**Reference Ranges:**
- **Male:** 41 - 53%
- **Female:** 36 - 46%

**Clinical Significance:**

| Status | Interpretation |
|--------|----------------|
| **Low** | Indicates reduced oxygen-carrying capacity, anemia, blood loss |
| **High** | Indicates blood thickening, dehydration, polycythemia |

**Relationship to HGB:** HCT ≈ HGB × 3 (approximate rule)

---

### 4. Mean Corpuscular Volume (MCV)

**Function:** Average size of red blood cells.

**Units:** fL (femtoliters)

**Reference Range:** 80 - 100 fL

**Clinical Significance:**

| Classification | MCV Range | Associated Conditions |
|----------------|-----------|----------------------|
| **Microcytic** | < 80 fL | Iron deficiency anemia, thalassemia, chronic disease |
| **Normocytic** | 80-100 fL | Normal, or acute blood loss, hemolytic anemia |
| **Macrocytic** | > 100 fL | B12/folate deficiency, liver disease, alcoholism |

---

### 5. Mean Corpuscular Hemoglobin (MCH)

**Function:** Average amount of hemoglobin per red blood cell.

**Units:** pg (picograms)

**Reference Range:** 27 - 34 pg

**Clinical Significance:**

| Status | Interpretation |
|--------|----------------|
| **Low** | Hypochromic anemia (pale cells), iron deficiency |
| **High** | Macrocytic anemia, B12/folate deficiency |

---

### 6. Mean Corpuscular Hemoglobin Concentration (MCHC)

**Function:** Average concentration of hemoglobin in red blood cells.

**Units:** g/dL

**Reference Range:** 32 - 36 g/dL

**Clinical Significance:**

| Status | Associated Conditions |
|--------|----------------------|
| **Low** | Iron deficiency anemia, thalassemia |
| **High** | Spherocytosis, severe dehydration |

---

### 7. Red Cell Distribution Width (RDW)

**Function:** Measure of variation in red blood cell size.

**Units:** % (coefficient of variation)

**Reference Range:** 11.5 - 14.5%

**Clinical Significance:**

| Status | Interpretation |
|--------|----------------|
| **Low** | Uniform cell size (rare, usually not significant) |
| **High** | Mixed cell populations, iron deficiency, B12 deficiency, recent transfusion |

**Diagnostic Value:**
- High RDW + Low MCV = Iron deficiency
- High RDW + High MCV = B12/folate deficiency
- Normal RDW + Low MCV = Thalassemia

## ⚪ White Blood Cell (WBC) Parameters

### 8. White Blood Cell Count (WBC)

**Function:** Cells that fight infection and disease.

**Units:** ×10³/µL (thousands per microliter)

**Reference Range:** 4.0 - 11.0 ×10³/µL

**Clinical Significance:**

| Status | Possible Causes |
|--------|-----------------|
| **Low (Leukopenia)** | Viral infections, bone marrow failure, chemotherapy, autoimmune disorders |
| **High (Leukocytosis)** | Bacterial infections, inflammation, leukemia, stress, pregnancy |

---

### 9. Lymphocytes (LYMP)

**Function:** Protect against viral infections and produce antibodies.

**Units:** % (percentage of total WBC)

**Reference Range:** 20 - 40%

**Clinical Significance:**

| Status | Associated Conditions |
|--------|----------------------|
| **Low (Lymphopenia)** | HIV/AIDS, immunodeficiency, chemotherapy, stress |
| **High (Lymphocytosis)** | Viral infections (mononucleosis), chronic lymphocytic leukemia |

---

### 10. Monocytes (MONO)

**Function:** Large white blood cells that fight bacteria and remove dead cells.

**Units:** % (percentage of total WBC)

**Reference Range:** 2 - 8%

**Clinical Significance:**

| Status | Associated Conditions |
|--------|----------------------|
| **Low** | Bone marrow disorders (rare) |
| **High** | Chronic infections (TB, endocarditis), inflammatory diseases, monocytic leukemia |

---

## 🟡 Platelet Parameters

### 11. Platelet Count (PLT)

**Function:** Cells responsible for blood clotting.

**Units:** ×10³/µL (thousands per microliter)

**Reference Range:** 150 - 450 ×10³/µL

**Clinical Significance:**

| Status | Possible Conditions |
|--------|---------------------|
| **Low (Thrombocytopenia)** | Bleeding disorders, bone marrow failure, immune destruction (ITP), chemotherapy |
| **High (Thrombocytosis)** | Infection, inflammatory diseases, iron deficiency, myeloproliferative disorders |

**Critical Values:**
- < 50,000: Risk of spontaneous bleeding
- < 20,000: Severe bleeding risk
- \> 1,000,000: Risk of thrombosis

---

### 12. Mean Platelet Volume (MPV)

**Function:** Average size of platelets.

**Units:** fL (femtoliters)

**Reference Range:** 7.5 - 11.5 fL

**Clinical Significance:**

| Status | Interpretation |
|--------|----------------|
| **Low** | Bone marrow underproduction, chemotherapy |
| **High** | Bone marrow overproduction, platelet destruction, large young platelets |

**Clinical Use:**
- High MPV + Low PLT = Increased platelet destruction
- Low MPV + Low PLT = Decreased platelet production

---

### 13. Platelet Distribution Width (PDW)

**Function:** Variation in platelet size.

**Units:** fL

**Reference Range:** 9 - 14 fL

**Clinical Significance:**

| Status | Associated Conditions |
|--------|----------------------|
| **Low** | Uniform platelet size |
| **High** | Platelet activation, bone marrow disorders |

---

### 14. Plateletcrit (PCT)

**Function:** Percentage of blood volume occupied by platelets.

**Units:** % (percentage)

**Reference Range:** 0.19 - 0.39%

**Clinical Significance:**

| Status | Correlation |
|--------|-------------|
| **Low** | Correlates with low platelet count |
| **High** | Correlates with high platelet count or large platelets |

---

## 🩺 Clinical Patterns

### Common Anemia Patterns

| Anemia Type | HGB | MCV | MCHC | MCH | RDW |
|-------------|-----|-----|------|-----|-----|
| **Iron Deficiency** | ↓ | ↓ | ↓ | ↓ | ↑ |
| **B12/Folate Deficiency** | ↓ | ↑ | N | ↑ | ↑ |
| **Thalassemia** | ↓ | ↓ | ↓ | ↓ | N |
| **Hemolytic** | ↓ | N/↑ | N | N | ↑ |
| **Chronic Disease** | ↓ | N/↓ | N/↓ | N | N |

Legend: ↓ = Low, ↑ = High, N = Normal

---

### Infection Patterns

| Infection Type | WBC | Neutrophils | Lymphocytes |
|----------------|-----|-------------|-------------|
| **Bacterial** | ↑ | ↑ | N/↓ |
| **Viral** | N/↓ | N/↓ | ↑ |
| **Parasitic** | ↑ | N | ↑ eosinophils |

## ⚠️ Critical Values

Values requiring immediate medical attention:

| Parameter | Critical Low | Critical High |
|-----------|--------------|---------------|
| **HGB** | < 6.0 g/dL | > 20.0 g/dL |
| **WBC** | < 2.0 ×10³/µL | > 30.0 ×10³/µL |
| **PLT** | < 50 ×10³/µL | > 1000 ×10³/µL |
